IDA is proud to announce the Data Discovery Challenge for 2015! As Singapore’s infocomm industry champion, IDA is looking for forward-looking solutions that will help open Singapore’s wealth of business data to discover never-before seen insights! Organised together with UP SINGAPORE, this challenge focuses on Opening Data, Driving Innovation, Enabling Smart Nation!

The Challenge seeks to encourage a wide range of data mashups of public and private datasets to unlock the untapped advantages of private data and improve how Singapore operates, encouraging smarter enterprises, achieving a Smart Nation.



Opening Data, Driving Innovation, Enabling Smart Nation

A Smart Nation is one where government can better serve its citizens by leveraging data and technology to better understand and anticipate their needs thereby adapting the services it provides. Enterprises will also be to make better, more informed business decisions, identify new business opportunities and develop new products and services.

Supported by sophisticated data analytics this data will provide insights on people mobility to assist transport planners and operations staff to provide delivery of timely services, connected buildings’ energy usage can be monitored and maximized for efficiency, citizen safety can be monitored and anticipated as well as an integrated health system for patient record management and remote delivery of medical assistance.

IDA now wants to ensure that private datasets are made more accessible to a wide range of potential users to enable mashups both with government datasets and other private datasets, expanding the supply of open data. In 2013, McKinsey & Co estimated “that seven sectors (globally) could generate more than $3 trillion a year in additional value as a result of open data”. The opportunities in Singapore alone are considerable.

Touted by many observers as the “New Oil” of the 21st Century, data presents all those wiling to explore its great rewards. Join the Challenge and see what value you can unlock for your organization and propel Singapore along its discovery path to become a Smart Nation.



Why Join?

We’re inviting all members of the business community, whether individuals, start-ups, SMEs or MNCs to participate, as this is a great opportunity for data scientists, analysts, data enthusiasts, technology solution providers or app developers to showcase their data credentials and develop opportunities for themselves.

Explore the datasets from the Federated Dataset Registry to derive socio-economic and environmental benefits for the nation.
The insights developed by combining multiple datasets, are to be rendered in a workable prototype that can


  • Increase efficiency or productivity of an

  • Develop new revenue streams or business models, or

  • Improve the way we live, work, learn and interact.

Participants are required to tap on at least one private dataset from Federated Dataset Registry to integrate with private and public datasets from other sources to develop useful applications, innovation of new products & services or even foster new business models.

Such applications will provide opportunities for the developers and organisations involved while enabling smarter enterprises and smarter citizen engagement for a Smart Nation.

We are encouraging participants to mash-up datasets from IDA’s Dataset Registry with public datasets to develop data visualisations, mobile/ web apps or any predictive dashboards.

This is an opportunity for data scientists, visualisers and designers to unearth meaningful insights from the wealth of corporate and social data that exists in Singapore.

FAQ

Can I submit more than 1 idea?

Yes, you can submit more than 1 idea by adding a one line summary on your idea in the comments section during each submission.


What if I submit more than once for the same idea (as I had to make some changes)?

We will take your latest submission as the final entry for that particular idea.


What are the submission requirements?

Submissions and their appropriate insights are to be rendered in a workable protoype that can
- Increase efficiency or productivity of an organization
- Develop new revenue streams or business models or
- Improve the way we live, work, learn, play and interact.


Are the participants free to use any technologies?

Yes, you are free to use any tool or technology. But note that all entries must be submitted in a digital format.


Are the participants free to use external datasets?

Yes, you are free to use other other, non-government datasets. In fact, we encourage you to be creative in gathering these other datasets. You shall be responsible for the appropriate use of non-government datasets, subject to the originators’ terms and conditions. Participants must remember to use at least one government dataset from IDA’s Dataset Registry.


Is attendance at the workshops mandatory?

Attendance is not mandatory, but is highly encouraged. It will be a useful orientation to data visualisation, storytelling and design, and a good help to get you started. It is also an excellent opportunity for you to meet like-minded people.


Why must my visualisation include the use of government data?

Every minute millions of data is being collected all over the world with the key to so many insights and discoveries that could change the way we do the simplest to most complex tasks. The Singapore Government holds a large amount of data that is now publicly available for research and analysis. Examples of government data can be found in the Federated Dataset Registry. Your visualisation needs to include at least one government dataset.
